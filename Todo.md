3. AI-Assisted Question Parsing & Pricing
Use AI to make posting bounties frictionless.

How it works: A student uploads a blurry photo of a NEET Biology diagram. You send the image to an AI model (like Gemini or OpenAI) in the background. The AI extracts the text, tags the subject (e.g., #Genetics), rates the difficulty (1-10), and suggests a fair bounty price based on the current market rate.

Why it's extraordinary: It saves the student from typing out complex math formulas and ensures mentors are paid fairly for incredibly hard questions.

4. Solution "Duels" & Community Voting
Instead of locking a bounty to the first person who claims it, turn it into a competition.

How it works: Multiple mentors can submit a "blurred" preview of their solution. The student (or the community) votes on whose explanation looks the cleanest or most thorough. The winner gets the bounty.

Why it's extraordinary: It forces mentors to provide high-quality, beautifully written explanations rather than just scribbling the final answer to grab the cash quickly.

5. Video "Shorts" Solutions
A PDF or image of a handwritten solution is great, but a 60-second video is better.

How it works: Allow mentors to upload a quick screen-recording or a phone video of them explaining the concept on a whiteboard.

Why it's extraordinary: It mimics the TikTok/Reels format that students are already addicted to. You could even compile the best video solutions into a searchable "TikTok for JEE/NEET" library that students can browse.

6. "Bounty Streaks" with Multipliers
Gamify the mentor's earning potential.

How it works: If a mentor solves 3 bounties perfectly in 24 hours, they enter a "Hot Streak." Any bounty they solve while on a hot streak gets a 1.2x payout bonus (subsidized by the platform to encourage high engagement).
1. The "Bounty Board" (Micro-Mentoring)
Instead of booking a full 1-hour session, a student who is stuck on a single, brutal physics problem or a specific UPSC essay prompt can post it on a public "Bounty Board."

How it works: Students attach a small bounty (e.g., ₹20 or platform coins) to a question.

The Hook: Approved mentors (or even top-tier students) can claim the bounty by providing a verified, high-quality video or text explanation. It creates a fast-paced, gig-economy style dynamic for quick doubt resolution.

2. RPG-Style Interactive Skill Trees
Standard syllabuses are boring. Turn the JEE/NEET/UPSC syllabus into a video game skill tree.

How it works: Instead of checking off boxes, students "unlock" nodes (e.g., unlocking "Rotational Mechanics" only after passing a mini-quiz on "Center of Mass").

The Hook: You can tie this directly into your new badge system. Completing a whole branch gives them a specific mastery badge. Mentors can look at a student's skill tree before a session to instantly know their weak points.

3. Live Co-Study "Pomodoro" Lounges
Studying for competitive exams is incredibly lonely.

How it works: Create a real-time "Study Lounge" route where students join a silent, virtual room. It features a synchronized Pomodoro timer (e.g., 50 mins focus, 10 mins break), an embedded lo-fi beats player, and a live list of who else is currently studying in the room.

The Hook: Add a "Focus Streak" tracker. If a student leaves the tab during the 50-minute focus block, they lose their daily streak.

4. "Roast My Prep" / Strategy Audits
Students are obsessed with knowing if their timetable is optimal.

How it works: Students submit their daily schedule, mock test scores, and current strategy. Mentors offer a 15-minute async video recording (or a written teardown) "roasting" or auditing their strategy, pointing out fatal flaws or areas to optimize.

The Hook: It’s a lower-commitment service for mentors to sell compared to hourly tutoring, and highly shareable for students.

5. Peer-to-Peer Flashcard Battles (1v1)
Tap into the competitive nature of these exams.

How it works: A global matchmaking system where two students are paired up for a rapid-fire, 60-second quiz on rote-memorization topics (e.g., Inorganic Chemistry exceptions, standard integration formulas, UPSC historical dates).

The Hook: Winner takes points, loser drops on the global leaderboard. It’s a great way to keep users opening the app just for 5 minutes while commuting or taking a break.
